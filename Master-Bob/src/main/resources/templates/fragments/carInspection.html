Car inspection

Car inspection is a routine procedure that ensures your vehicle is safe, reliable, and compliant with legal standards. During an inspection, various aspects of the car are examined, including its engine performance, brake system, lights, tires, and emissions. This check-up helps identify any potential issues before they become serious problems, maintaining the car's efficiency and safety on the road. Regular inspections are crucial for preventing breakdowns and ensuring your vehicle meets the required safety and environmental regulations.

What does the inspector do?

Inspectors conduct a comprehensive evaluation of your vehicle using a detailed checklist, examining both the interior and exterior thoroughly. They employ specialized tools and equipment to assess various components, often going beyond the standard dealer checks. CarExamer provides a detailed comparison list of their vehicle inspections, highlighting key areas that are typically covered, including:

  • Engine Compartment
  • Electrical Controls
  • Wheels and tires
  • Suspension and steering
  • Transmission
  • Body exterior
  • Cooling systems

Car inspection costs

The cost of a car inspection can vary widely depending on several factors, including the type of vehicle, its age, and the scope of the inspection. For passenger vehicles, the cost ranges from 30 to 60 EUR. For motorcycles, the cost ranges from 20 to 40 EUR. For cargo vehicles (up to 12 tons) and buses (up to 5 tons), the cost ranges from 45 to 90 EUR. For cargo vehicles (over 12 tons) and buses (over 5 tons), the cost ranges from 60 to 100 EUR. It's important to note that these prices are for informational purposes and can vary based on the specific requirements of your vehicle and the service provider.
