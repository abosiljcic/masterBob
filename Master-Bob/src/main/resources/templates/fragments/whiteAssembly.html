White assembly

One of the final stages of finishing work is the white assembly in the bathroom, toilet, and kitchen. The term "white assembly" refers to the installation of sanitary ceramics, which are coated with a layer of glaze. It involves connecting all the sanitary fixtures, including the washbasin, sink, bathtub, shower tray, toilet, bidet, and other ceramic elements, as well as fittings that complete the hot and cold water installations at designated points. Completing the white assembly signifies the near end of the construction process, even if some final touches remain. White assembly is crucial for two main reasons: it not only enhances the interior decor but also ensures a high level of safety for the occupants. This requires not only professional expertise but also the correct selection of seals, insulating tapes, joints, and silicones. In practice, white assembly also includes the installation of kitchen or bathroom faucets and the laying of ceramic tiles.

When is white assembly performed?

White assembly is typically performed at the end of construction, once all installations are complete and there's assurance that the ceramics will not be damaged or soiled. By this stage, the interiors are usually cleaned, with only the sanitary installations left to enable the use of the toilet and kitchen. White assembly is crucial as it affects the functionality of the rooms, their overall appearance, and ensures long-term use without issues.

White assembly cost

The prices of white installation depend mainly on the region where the service is performed and the individual rates of individual specialists. On average, you should expect the following installation costs: bathtub - approx. 70 EUR (if it is covered with ceramic tiles, the cost will be even higher), shower cabin with shower tray and tap - approx. 55 EUR (may be lower/higher depending on the type of cabin), bidet, toilet bowl or washbasin - approx. 20 EUR per item, and fittings installation is approx. 10 EUR for each tap.
