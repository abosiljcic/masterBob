Roof painting

The choice to paint a roof can stem from various motivations. Typically, it is driven by the need for regular maintenance. Roofs endure constant exposure to fluctuating weather conditions and air pollution, which can lead to deterioration, particularly with metal roofs prone to corrosion and peeling. Painting the roof may also be part of a desire to update its appearance or as a component of a broader home renovation project that includes changing the style and layout. Since replacing a roof covering is considerably more costly than painting, refreshing a roof that is still in good condition can be a cost-effective solution.

Additional roof painting services

Painting a roof typically requires extra steps beyond the actual painting process. Preparation work includes repairing defects and filling gaps, in addition to surface cleaning. Before a professional begins painting, they must thoroughly clean the roof, which involves removing old paint, rust, mold, and fungi. Additionally, the roof needs to be properly treated with a primer. Metal roofs are the simplest to prepare and paint, while ceramic and concrete tiles require more time and effort.

Roof Painting Costs

When budgeting for a roof painting project, it’s important to account for not just the cost of the paint and the actual painting, but also any additional work required. This includes preparing and cleaning the roof, as well as applying a primer. Prices are typically calculated per square meter. Cleaning and priming the roof costs approximately 2-4/m² EUR, depending on the material. Painting metal roof tiles generally costs around 2/m² EUR, whereas ceramic or concrete roof tiles are priced between 4/m² EUR. The range of paint prices on the market is quite broad, so if you prefer not to make the choice yourself, seeking advice from a specialist can be beneficial.
