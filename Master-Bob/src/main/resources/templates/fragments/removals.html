Removals

As we become increasingly mobile, changing addresses more frequently, finding a new place can be both a significant challenge and a major life transition. However, the moving process itself often proves to be troublesome. Many people underestimate the amount of belongings they have accumulated, leading them to believe they can manage the move independently. Yet, as the packing progresses and the number of boxes rapidly increases, it becomes clear that professional assistance is necessary. A transport company can handle the logistics of moving your belongings from one location to another. In contrast, a moving company offers a more comprehensive service, handling not only the transport but also the packing, loading, unloading, and even the disposal of unwanted items.

What services does a moving company offer?

Today, there is a wide range of moving and transport companies available, each offering different services. Transport companies generally provide a basic service focused solely on renting a vehicle with a driver. In contrast, professional moving companies offer a much more comprehensive range of services. They supply boxes, rent out plastic containers, assist with packing, and handle loading, transport, and unloading. These companies not only provide the necessary transportation but also supply the required workforce. They are well-equipped to manage large-scale moves and can transport a variety of items, including household appliances, RTV equipment, furniture, antiques, pianos, safes, and other unique pieces of home and apartment furnishings.

Removals costs

Most moving companies don't publish standard price lists and instead set their rates based on individual circumstances. Several factors influence the cost of hiring professionals for a move:

  • Scale of the Move

    • The extent of the move significantly affects the price. More items to transport typically result in higher costs, as a larger vehicle or additional team members may be required. Transporting oversized items, such as large musical instruments or safes, is often charged separately as an additional service.

  • Location

    • Rates are generally higher in larger cities compared to smaller towns. The location also impacts the time needed to complete the move. Since transport services are often billed by the hour, moving in congested areas can lead to increased costs due to traffic delays. To save money, consider scheduling your move outside of peak hours.

  • Property Location

    • The specific location of your property also affects the cost. Moving from a high floor in a building without an elevator can increase expenses, as it takes more time to handle items, particularly large ones. Parking availability is another factor—if there's no convenient parking or access issues, loading and unloading will take longer, impacting the final cost. Remember that you can often negotiate the price of moving services. With the competitive nature of the moving industry, it’s advisable to compare offers from several companies to find the best deal. This approach can lead to significant savings.
