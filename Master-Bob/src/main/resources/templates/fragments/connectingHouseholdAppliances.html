Connecting household appliances

The term "household appliances" or "household products" encompasses all the equipment we use daily. This includes both large appliances, such as refrigerators, dishwashers, washing machines, dryers, microwaves, cookers, and ovens, as well as smaller items like irons, grills, and toasters. While small household appliances are straightforward to set up by simply plugging them into an outlet, larger appliances often require additional installation, such as connecting to water or gas systems. For devices like washing machines, tumble dryers, dishwashers, and cookers, proper installation is crucial to avoid potential leaks in the pipes. Therefore, it is advisable to have these installations handled by professionals.

Why should household appliances be connected by professionals?

When installing a washing machine, it's crucial to place it in a location with not only an electrical outlet but also a cold water connection and a drainage system. The provided drain pipe and water connection hose typically measure between 120 and 150 cm in length, so consider this when positioning the machine. The washing machine must be connected to the water supply, with the hose securely attached to the valve using a metal ring. After this, the water drain pipe should be connected to the inlet. Additionally, two important steps must not be overlooked: leveling the machine to prevent movement during the spin cycle and removing transport locks from the drum and motor. Using the washing machine with these locks in place can cause damage. As you can see, setting up a washing machine involves more than just plugging it in.
